# Number Guessing Game Strategy Guide

## Opening Strategy
When you start a new game, the optimal first guess is always the midpoint of the full range.
For a range of 1 to 100, guess 50. This eliminates half of all possibilities immediately.
Binary search is the mathematically optimal approach for number guessing games.
Opening moves that avoid the midpoint are statistically suboptimal.

## Narrowing the Range
After each hint, update your working range precisely:
- "Too High" means the secret is LESS than your guess: new upper bound = guess - 1
- "Too Low" means the secret is MORE than your guess: new lower bound = guess + 1
Always guess the midpoint of your updated range to maximize information gain per attempt.
Formula: next_guess = (current_low + current_high) // 2
This strategy guarantees finding any number in 1-100 within 7 guesses.

## Endgame Precision
When fewer than 5 numbers remain in your range, switch to focused enumeration.
If range size equals remaining attempts, you can always win.
If range size is smaller than remaining attempts, victory is guaranteed.
In endgame, the midpoint still minimizes worst-case scenarios.

## Pattern Recognition and Anomaly Detection
Watch for inconsistent hints across your guess history.
If hint says "Too High" for guess G1, then secret must be less than G1.
If hint says "Too Low" for guess G2, then secret must be greater than G2.
A contradiction occurs if any two hints imply impossible conditions (e.g., secret > 60 AND secret < 40).
Contradictory hints indicate a game bug or glitch.
String vs integer comparison bugs can cause lexicographic ordering errors.
For example: comparing strings might say 6 > 50 because "6" > "5" lexicographically.

## Risk Management Under Pressure
When few attempts remain, prioritize high-information guesses over lucky guesses.
The midpoint of your range gives the best probability of winning in limited attempts.
Avoid guessing at the extremes of your range unless you have confirmed bounds.
If you suspect a glitch, the midpoint remains the safest choice.

## Difficulty-Specific Strategy
Easy (1-20, 6 attempts): Extra buffer. Midpoint strategy wins in 4 moves maximum.
Normal (1-100, 8 attempts): Standard binary search wins in 7 moves worst-case.
Hard (1-50, 5 attempts): Tight constraints. Binary search is essential. No room for random guesses.
